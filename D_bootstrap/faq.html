FAQ Frequently Asked Questions - Homeowner

+
What kind of background check do you carry out on the person?

All Elderhomeshare renters in Dublin, Ireland go through a vetting process; references are provided and followed up up by phone. In addition, Elderhomeshare meets with and questions potential renters on their back ground, lifestyle and routine in order to make the best possible match with a home owner

+
Does everyone have Garda Vetting?

Garda vetting of tenants is available at the request of the home owner

+
Does the person have a qualification to care for older people?

No. Elderhomeshare renters are not carers and are not obliged to carry out caring duties.

+
What if they are from another country - are they vetted in the country of origin?

No. Garda vetting covers their activities in Ireland

+
What if the person starts to take advantage of my home?

If you have any concerns about the behavior of the person in your home you can call Elderhomeshare who will contact the person or you can arrange for a call out to resolve the issue click here to review misconduct procedure.

+
Is there a trial period?

We suggest a trial period of one month so you can both decide if the living arrangement is working.

+
How much rent can I charge?

It is completely up to you depending on your priorities and circumstances. You may want to charge less in exchange for some practical help at home or more if you are just looking to generate revenue based on the extra rooms in your home. The most popular option is to charge 100 euro a month rent and bills in exchange for 8 to 10 hours a week in companionship and support at home.

+
Does the person contribute to the bills?

It is up to the homeowner to decide if they want a contribution towards the bills.

+
What can I expect of the person in regards to helping in the home?

Each arrangement is different with different requirements. Discussing your needs in the initial meeting will help to lay down the ground rules. The renter is not there to carry out lots of work or replace the role of a professional carer, but a healthy contribution would be expected.

FAQFrequently Asked Questions - Companion

+
What ID do I need to provide for vetting?

Elderhomeshare will need the following which is in line with Garda Vetting policy.
● 3 to 4 written references on formal company paper from professional bodies for example employer, university, place you volunteer.
● Copy of passport or drivers licence
● Current pay slip or P 60 or P45
● Proof of current address utility bill, bank statement, letter from employer confirming current address

+
Can I bring my friends into the house?

Some homeowners are happy for a tenant to have a guest over for tea or dinner once a week as an Elderhomeshare tenant socialising is done outside of the home as a general rule. A guest once a week for dinner or a chat over tea can be negotiated with the homeowner.

+
Can I have someone stay over?

Having people stay over is not part of the Elderhomeshare arrangement.

+
I'm in a relationship I want to spend two nights out of the house a week?

Arranging to have two nights out of the house each week would be considered a reasonable request.

+
Do I need to make contribution to the bills?

Contribution to the bills is arranged with the homeowner in the contracting stage. It is up to the homeowner and yourself if and what you contribute. Being careful in your use of electricity and water is a good idea in any regard as bills are expensive.

+
What is expected of me in exchange for the reduced rental price?

This differs from case to case and needs to be figured out in the agreement contract stage. Light housework, dinner twice a week, and small errands would be an example of an arrangement.

+
How much notice do I need to give when moving out?

Giving one month notice is advisable.

+
How much notice can I expect to be given?

Likewise receiving one month notice, however if a renter is behaving outside of the agreed upon contract such as bringing people into the house making excessive noise the Elderhomeshare contract can be terminated.

+
Is there a trial period to see if the living arrangement is working?

Yes, it is a good idea for both sides to test the arrangement with a one month’s getting to know you 'live in' arrangement.

+
What if the person I am sharing with becomes ill and needs a lot of help. Is that my resonsibility?

No, it is not your responsibility letting a family member of the person you live with know what is happening or contacting the local doctor is an appropriate intervention.

+
Can I go on Holidays when I am living in the home owners house?

Yes you can go on holidays giving some notice to the homeowner is suggested so they can plan to have an idea of your movements is a polite action we ask Elderhomeshare renters to do.
